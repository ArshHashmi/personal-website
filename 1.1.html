01 — About

I'm a first-year Mechanical Engineering student at Binghamton University's Watson College of Engineering. My work centers on applied design — taking problems from sketch to physical reality.

I've designed aerodynamic components, run CFD simulations in ANSYS Fluent, manufactured carbon fiber layups, welded EV chassis frames, built and competed with combat robots, and restored salvage vehicles from the ground up.

Outside of school I run Cosmic PCs, a custom PC building business I founded in 2022 — averaging 3–5 orders per week with a team of three. I also obsess over automotive electronics, vehicle diagnostics, and systems-level problem solving.

I'm actively seeking engineering internships where I can do real work on real systems. No busy work.

FSAE Nosecone — Aerodynamic Design

Designed the nosecone for Binghamton Motorsports' EV formula car in Fusion 360. Ran full CFD analysis in ANSYS Fluent — evaluated drag coefficients, pressure distribution, and velocity contours. Created DXF files for waterjet manufacturing and executed carbon fiber composite layup and post-processing.

002
Front Wing V2 — Downforce Optimization

Designed second-generation front wing in Fusion 360. Generated computational meshes with inflation layers to resolve boundary layer flow. Iterated through multiple geometry angles to maximize downforce and aerodynamic efficiency. Analyzed lift/drag coefficients and pressure/velocity distributions across conditions.

CFD Boundary Layer Fusion 360 Iterative Design

003
EV Go-Kart — Chassis Design & Fabrication

Designed the frame, chain guard, and brake caliper bracket for an electric go-kart chassis in Fusion 360. Validated component fitment, drivetrain alignment, and braking system integration through CAD assembly. Hands-on fabrication: MIG welding, notching, surface prep, and final assembly.

Chassis Design MIG Welding Fusion 360 EV Systems

004
Antweight Combat Robot — Full Build

Led full mechanical and electrical build of a 1 lb combat robot for university competition. Designed all parts in Fusion 360, 3D printed multiple iterations with tolerance validation, and integrated drive motors, radio systems, weapon motors, and batteries. Iterated based on stress testing and competition performance data.

Combat Robotics Soldering Fusion 360 Stress Testing

005
DIY Driving Simulator — CAN-BUS + G-Force System

Led a team of four to build an immersive driving simulator. Encoded CAN-BUS data via Arduino to display real-time in-game telemetry on a physical car dash cluster. Built a two-channel wind/g-force simulator that read in-game acceleration vectors and translated them to directional fan output for physical driver feedback.

Arduino CAN-BUS Systems Integration Team Lead
